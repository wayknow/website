Why ClearJSON Exists

A story of betrayal — and a trustworthy alternative

In early 2025, the JSON Formatter Chrome extension — with 2M+ users, 2,100+ reviews, and 10 years of open-source history — was sold to a new team. They closed the source, injected GiveFreely ads into web pages, tracked users via MaxMind GeoIP, and sent data to third-party servers. Hacker News exposed it. Two million users started looking for a replacement.

Five alternatives emerged within a year. All are free, open-source passion projects. None are commercialized — meaning no long-term maintenance commitment. ClearJSON fills that gap.
